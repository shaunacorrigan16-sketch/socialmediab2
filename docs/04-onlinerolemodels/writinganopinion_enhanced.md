## Introduction

How to write an opinion piece 📝

An opinion piece is a piece of writing that presents the author's perspective, thoughts, and judgments on a particular topic. It allows you to express your views while supporting them with evidence and logical reasoning.

An opinion piece can be divided into 3 parts:

1. **Introduction** - Hook the reader and state your position
2. **Main Body / Argument** - Develop your ideas with evidence
3. **Conclusion** - Summarize your key points

---

## Part 1: Introduction

### What to Include

In the introduction, you should include:

#### 1. A line introducing the topic

Examples:

- The topic of … has been widely discussed in recent years.
- In recent years, there has been a lot of debate about …
- Nowadays, many people have different opinions about …
- .... has become an important issue in modern society.

#### 2. A line briefly stating your opinion, without justification

Useful phrases:

- I believe…
- In my opinion…
- From my perspective…
- It seems to me that…
- I would argue that…

---

## Part 2: Main Body / Argument

### Developing Your Ideas

In this section, include your main argument(s). Provide examples or evidence to support your opinion. You can use the internet to research.

**Examples of supporting evidence:**

- A study found that 80% of students…
- According to research…
- For example, Facebook has over 2 billion daily users…
- Statistics show that…
- Recent surveys indicate that…

### Using Connectors

Connect your ideas logically:

- Furthermore…
- Moreover…
- However…
- In addition…
- On the other hand…

---

## Part 3: Conclusion

Finally, summarize your opinion in one or two lines. You can use the following connectors to introduce your conclusion:

- **Finally…**
- **In summary…**
- **To sum up…**
- **At the end of the day…**
- **In conclusion…**
- **To conclude…**

Make sure your conclusion reinforces your main argument without introducing new information.

## Writing Exercise ✍️

### Your Turn!

**Write a short opinion piece** with an introduction, main body, and conclusion about the following topic:

> **Are social media influencers good role models?**

### Requirements

Your text must meet the following criteria:

- **Length:** 100-120 words
- **At least 2 phrasal verbs** (e.g., look up to, set up, bring about, give up)
- **At least 3 modal verbs** (e.g., can, could, should, must, might, may)

### Phrasal Verbs to Consider

- Look up to (admire)
- Set up (establish)
- Bring about (cause)
- Give up (quit)
- Hang around (spend time)
- Show off (display proudly)
- Get by (manage)
- Turn to (seek help from)

### Modal Verbs to Consider

- **can** - ability or permission
- **could** - past ability or possibility
- **should** - obligation or advice
- **must** - strong necessity
- **might** - possibility
- **may** - permission or possibility
- **will** - future certainty
- **would** - conditional

## Tips for Success

### General Writing Tips

1. **Plan before you write** - Outline your main points
2. **Use clear language** - Avoid overly complex sentences
3. **Support your opinion** - Always provide evidence
4. **Stay on topic** - Don't go off on tangents
5. **Proofread** - Check for grammar and spelling errors
6. **Use transitions** - Connect your ideas smoothly
7. **Be respectful** - Even if expressing disagreement

### Checking Your Work

Before submitting your opinion piece, ask yourself:

- [ ] Does my introduction clearly state the topic?
- [ ] Is my opinion clearly stated?
- [ ] Do I have 2+ phrasal verbs?
- [ ] Do I have 3+ modal verbs?
- [ ] Is my conclusion strong and summarizing?
- [ ] Is the word count between 100-120 words?
- [ ] Are there any spelling or grammar mistakes?
