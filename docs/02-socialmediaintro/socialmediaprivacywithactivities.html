2. Modal Verbs ✍🏻

What is a modal verb?

A modal verb is a type of auxiliary verb used to add meaning to the main verb. They can express ability, possibility, advice , obligation or necessity.

The examples we are going to see today are:

Modal verb Used to express Function (Social Media Example)
can ability / permission Expressing what users are allowed to do: “You can report harmful content on most platforms.”
can’t inability / prohibition Expressing what is not allowed or not possible: “You can’t use some apps without age verification.”
could suggestion / possibility Making suggestions or talking about possibilities: “We could take a break from social media for a day.”
couldn’t past inability / impossibility Expressing something not possible in the past: “You couldn’t easily control privacy settings in early social media apps.”
should advice Giving recommendations: “You should think before you post online.”
shouldn’t advice / warning Warning against bad behaviour: “You shouldn’t share personal details publicly.”
must strong obligation / rule Expressing strong rules: “Users must follow community guidelines.”
mustn’t prohibition Expressing strong prohibition: “You mustn’t spread fake news or harmful content.”
